We Are Interested in Land-Ocean-Groundwater Science

Hello! I am a hydrogeologist focusing on scientific questions related to hydrogeology and biogeochemistry in critical zones such as intertidal aquifer and hyporheic zone. You could find my offical website on HKUST(gz).

We, LOG-Sci Group investigates the water and chemicals exchange, biogeochemical processes during the surface water - groundwater interaction in river basin systems and land-ocean interaction in coastal aquifer systems.

Multiple methods including isotopic tracing, remote sensing, laboratory analysis, field monitoring and numerical modelling are coupled to quantify the contribution and influences of groundwater discharge to the chemical budget and environment in river system and coastal seas.

Latest News

The Hyporheic Exchange Remarkably Influences Methane Dynamics and Effluxes in Rivers

Our study in the Pearl River Basin shows that methane levels in rivers vary significantly between the wet and dry seasons, with higher concentrations in the wet season. Methane emissions from the basin contribute more than 50% of the warming potential of CO2 emissions. The hyporheic zone plays a key role in methane production and emissions, acting as both a hotspot and a conduit for methane from groundwater to rivers, especially in larger rivers. Understanding methane dynamics in rivers is crucial for addressing global greenhouse gas challenges.
